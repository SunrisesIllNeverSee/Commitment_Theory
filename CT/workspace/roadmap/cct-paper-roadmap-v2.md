## Part I: The Legal Track

### Root Node

**L-000 | Propositions of Commitment Conservation for Legal Applications**

A short deposit that stakes the claim and maps the legal research program. Not a full paper. A signpost.

- Format: 1,500–2,000 words
- Structure: Five to six numbered propositions stating CCT's legal claims, followed by a one-paragraph description of each paper in the legal track
- Venue: SSRN deposit + Zenodo DOI
- Dependency: P-000 (CCT Propositions Prospectus) deposited first
- Co-author: Not required
- Timing: Deposit before or concurrent with L-001 submission

**Core Propositions:**

1. Legal meaning carries a commitment kernel — obligations, prohibitions, modal constraints — that is operationally measurable.
2. Under governed transformation, the commitment kernel is conserved. This is an empirically tested conservation law, not a guideline or aspiration.
3. Under ungoverned transformation, the commitment kernel decays monotonically. No existing AI governance framework addresses this entropic force.
4. A commitment conservation requirement — the legal standard that AI systems applying legal provisions must demonstrably preserve the commitment kernel — is a necessary prerequisite for due process, evidentiary reliability, and antidiscrimination enforcement.
5. The Conservation Law of Commitment is the only governance foundation backed by a falsifiable physical law with an empirical record. All other AI governance frameworks operate as policy without physics.
6. Pluralism is conserved, not erased. The framework preserves interpretive dispute. It does not resolve it.

**Paper map:** One paragraph each for L-001 through L-010.
