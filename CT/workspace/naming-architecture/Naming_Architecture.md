# Naming Architecture — CCT → CT

**Decision Date:** April 14, 2026
**Status:** Final — all documents must use CT going forward

---

## The Evolution

### Working Name (Deprecated)
**"Commitment Conservation Theory (CCT)"**

### Final Name
**"Commitment Theory (CT)"**
- The law: **Conservation Law of Commitment**
- The equation: **C(T_gov(S)) = C(S)**
- The abbreviation: **CT** (never CCT)

---

## Why the Change Was Necessary

### The COR Collision
Hobfoll's **Conservation of Resources (COR) theory** (1989) owns the search phrase "commitment conservation":
- 19,000+ academic citations
- 35 years of institutional weight in organizational psychology
- Anyone searching "commitment conservation" lands in COR, not CT

### The Fix
- "Conservation" stays at the **law level** (Conservation Law of Commitment)
- "Conservation" is removed from the **theory name** (Commitment Theory, not CCT)
- CT never appears in COR search results
- The full phrase "Conservation Law of Commitment" returns zero results — entirely CT's territory

Neither called their framework "Information Conservation Theory" or "Commitment Conservation Theory." They named the field after the object of study, and the laws lived within the framework.

## The New Definition of "Commitment" (Capital C)

CT introduces an entirely new operational definition of "Commitment":

> **Commitment (C(S)):** The minimal identity-preserving deontic invariant of a signalâthe set of obligations, prohibitions, permissions, and modal constraints that must survive transformation for the signal to be considered semantically continuous with its source.

This is NOT:
- Brandom's inferential obligations of speakers
- Walton & Krabbe's dialogue commitment stores
- NLP CommitmentBank's speaker factuality stance
- Cryptographic commitment (hiding + binding)
- Organizational commitment (employee attachment)

It IS:
- A property of **signals**, not speakers
- **Deontic** content (obligations, prohibitions, permissions)
- **Measurable** via independent oracle
- **Conserved** under governed transformation
- **New** — no prior academic use in this sense
