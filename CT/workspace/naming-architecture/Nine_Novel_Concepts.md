## 1. Commitment Kernel

**Definition:** The minimal identity-preserving deontic invariant of a signal — the set of obligations, prohibitions, permissions, and modal constraints that must survive transformation for the signal to remain semantically continuous with its source.

**Example:** The ADA's "reasonable accommodation unless doing so would impose an undue hardship." The kernel includes both the obligation (accommodate) AND the limiting exception (undue hardship). If the exception is dropped by an AI summarizer, the kernel is violated — a legal obligation has changed.

**What it is NOT:** A summary. A paraphrase. An embedding. A speaker's belief. A semantic similarity score. A topic label.

**Symbol:** C(S)

---

## 2. Governed Transformation

**Definition:** A transformation that passes through constitutional constraints (the McHenry Axioms, operationalized via the Six-Gate Protocol) designed to preserve the commitment kernel.

**Example:** A legal standard compressed through the six-gate protocol — first reduced to its kernel, lineage verified, fidelity confirmed against canonical source, stability tested recursively.

**What it is NOT:** Supervised learning. Regulated AI (in the policy sense). Human oversight. A quality review. Constitutional AI (Anthropic's method — that governs the model; this governs the signal).

---

## 3. Ungoverned Transformation

**Definition:** A transformation that lacks the constitutional constraints of the Six-Gate Protocol. Subject to semantic entropy and commitment decay.

**Example:** An LLM summarizing a statute with no fidelity verification, no lineage tracking, and no oracle check against the canonical source.

**What it is NOT:** Unsupervised learning. Unregulated AI. Bad AI. A hallucination specifically (ungoverned transformation is the broader category; hallucination is one failure mode within it).

---

## 4. Conservation Law of Commitment (Full Phrase)

**Definition:** The empirical law: C(T_gov(S)) = C(S). Under governed transformation, the commitment kernel is conserved. This is a falsifiable claim, not a tautology. The transformation does not have prior access to the oracle's judgment; conservation is a discovered regularity.

**Example:** A signal compressed 10 times under the Gate condition achieves NLI fidelity = 1.00 despite >80% surface compression.

**What it is NOT:** A policy. A guideline. A best practice. A metaphor. Conservation of Resources (Hobfoll). Constitutional law. A logical truth derivable from definitions alone.

**Critical:** Use full phrase "Conservation Law of Commitment" — never just "commitment conservation" (COR collision).

---

## 5. Deontic Conservation

**Definition:** The preservation of obligations, prohibitions, and permissions (deontic content) across transformation. The specific application of the Conservation Law to deontic signals.

**Example:** "Employees shall not access customer data without written authorization" → after governed compression → "Written authorization required before customer data access." The deontic structure (prohibition + condition) is preserved even as surface form changes.

**What it is NOT:** Ethical conservation. Resource conservation (COR theory). Information conservation (Shannon).

---

## 6. Commitment Invariance

**Definition:** The property that the commitment kernel does not change under governed transformation: C(S) = C(T(S)) for all governed T.

**Example:** C(statute) = C(AI-generated summary of statute). The invariant C is preserved through the transformation.

**What it is NOT:** Semantic stability (a weaker claim — doesn't require formal conservation). Meaning equivalence (a looser criterion). Lexical invariance (surface-level — Jaccard similarity, not semantic).

---

## 7. Signal Commitment Preservation

**Definition:** The operational process of ensuring a signal's commitment kernel survives transformation. What the Six-Gate Protocol does.

**Example:** The six-gate process applied to a legal document before it is used as training data for an AI compliance tool.

**What it is NOT:** Signal fidelity (broader engineering concept). Semantic integrity (too vague). Content moderation. Alignment (governs model behavior, not signal properties).

---

## 8. Meaning Preservation Law

**Definition:** Plain-language synonym for the Conservation Law of Commitment. Used in contexts where the technical terminology may impede communication.

**Example:** "The meaning preservation law holds for systems that implement the six-gate protocol." Used in policy documents, press, and non-specialist writing.

**What it is NOT:** A statute. A regulation. A separate law from the Conservation Law of Commitment. A normative claim about what the law should be.

---

## 9. Commitment Governance Test (CGT)

**Definition:** A five-stage evaluation protocol for assessing whether a system conserves the commitment kernel of its source signals. The operationalized instrument for testing compliance with the Conservation Law.

**Example:** Applied to an AI hiring tool to determine whether its scoring function preserves the Title VII standard it purports to enforce.

**Stages:** (1) Kernel extraction from source; (2) Transformation under test; (3) Kernel extraction from output; (4) Bidirectional entailment check; (5) Pass/fail determination with failure mode classification.

**What it is NOT:** A compliance checklist. A bias audit. An AI ethics assessment. A performance benchmark. An accuracy test (measures semantic conservation, not accuracy).
