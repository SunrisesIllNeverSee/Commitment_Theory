## Platform Rules of Thumb

**If it's legal → SSRN first, Zenodo backup.**
Legal scholars, law review editors, and policy researchers search SSRN. Your legal papers need to be there.

**If it's science/CS/linguistics → Zenodo first, ResearchGate for distribution.**
Zenodo gives you the DOI and license control. ResearchGate gives you the SEO.

**If it's governance/AI safety → add LessWrong.**
A shorter, punchier version on LessWrong reaches the AI safety community directly and has the highest SEO for governance-related searches.

**Everything goes on ResearchGate as a distribution copy.**
It's free, you keep your rights, and it has strong Google indexing across all fields.

**signomy.xyz is your canonical home.**
The brand version of every major document lives here. P-000, L-000, and any public-facing architecture documents.

**Never use Preprints.org.**
Forced CC-BY. Incompatible with your IP protection requirements.

## License Recommendations by Document Type

| Document Type | Recommended License | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| P-000, L-000 (propositions/roadmaps) | CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0 | Visible but protected. No commercial reuse, no derivatives. |
| Empirical papers (L-002, CL-001, CL-002) | CC-BY-NC 4.0 | Allow academic reuse, block commercial exploitation. |
| Policy briefs (L-004) | CC-BY 4.0 | Maximum distribution. You want policymakers sharing this freely. |
| MO§ES™ Architecture Paper | All Rights Reserved | Proprietary architecture. Patent-pending. Lock it down. |
| GOV-001 (governance gap article) | CC-BY-NC 4.0 | You want it cited widely but not commercially exploited. |
| Legal theory papers (L-003, L-007, L-008) | CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0 | Protect the theoretical contribution. |
