## Part I: The Legal Track

### Root Node

**L-000 | Propositions of Commitment Conservation Theory for Legal Applications**

A legal-specific companion to the CCT Propositions prospectus. States the theory's legal claims as numbered propositions, maps the research program, and serves as the single citable root for all legal papers that follow. Every legal paper in this track cites L-000.

- Format: Research prospectus (8,000–10,000 words)
- Venue: SSRN deposit + Zenodo DOI + signomy.xyz
- Dependency: Requires CCT Propositions prospectus (P-000) to exist first
- Co-author value: High (legal collaborator strengthens jurisprudential framing)
- Timing: After SLRO submission, after empirical legal study generates legal-specific data

Core propositions would include:

- Legal meaning carries a commitment kernel (obligations, prohibitions, modal constraints) that is operationally measurable.
- Under governed transformation, the commitment kernel is conserved. Under ungoverned transformation, it decays.
- Semantic continuity is a prerequisite for due process, evidentiary reliability, and antidiscrimination enforcement in AI-mediated legal systems.
- The Six-Gate Governance Protocol provides an operational mechanism for achieving semantic continuity in legal contexts.
- Pluralism is conserved, not erased — the framework preserves interpretive dispute, it does not resolve it.
