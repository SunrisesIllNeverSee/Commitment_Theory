## The Stress Tests (Five Anticipated Attacks)

1. "Calling it a 'conservation law' is premature" → It's a specific scientific category (invariant under transformation), not a general law claim
2. "EU AI Act > a Zenodo preprint" → Different categories: regulation tells you what to do; the conservation law tells you what happens
3. "Constitutional AI has empirical evidence too" → CT identifies a *conserved quantity* (invariant claim), not just effectiveness (correlation claim)
4. "You conflate governance with science" → Both needed: science describes the physics; regulation prescribes the response
5. "Proprietary = rent-seeking" → Law is open; machine is proprietary (like Daubert: principle open, instruments proprietary)
