## Abstract

This prospectus deposits six foundational propositions that stake Commitment Theory's legal claim and map the Legal_Theory research program (L-001 through L-010). CT provides the first formal framework for the governance of semantic commitment under AI transformation, grounded in a falsifiable physical law with an empirical record. The six propositions establish the legal relevance of that framework: (1) Legal meaning carries a commitment kernel — obligations, prohibitions, and modal constraints — that is operationally measurable using the CT harness. (2) Under governed transformation, the commitment kernel is conserved — an empirical result, not a design aspiration. (3) Under ungoverned transformation, the commitment kernel decays monotonically — and no existing AI governance framework addresses this decay in legal contexts. (4) A commitment conservation requirement is a necessary prerequisite for due process (Mathews v. Eldridge), evidentiary reliability (FRE 901, Daubert), and antidiscrimination enforcement (Griggs v. Duke Power Co.) in AI-mediated legal processes. (5) CT is the first governance foundation for legal AI backed by an empirically falsifiable conservation law with a public empirical record. (6) Pluralism is conserved — CT preserves interpretive dispute and doctrinal disagreement; it does not resolve contested questions of statutory meaning, only ensures that the contested question is preserved rather than collapsed by AI transformation. Each proposition is followed by a one-paragraph description of each paper in the L-001 through L-010 program.

## Key Claims

- Legal semantic objects carry a commitment kernel (obligations, prohibitions, modal constraints) that is operationally measurable — legal meaning is not exempt from CT's framework.
- Under ungoverned AI transformation, legal commitment kernels decay monotonically; this decay is not hypothetical but empirically demonstrated and maps directly onto established legal harm categories (due process, evidentiary reliability, antidiscrimination enforcement).
- A commitment conservation requirement is legally necessary — it is implied by existing doctrine, not imposed by CT from outside the law.
- CT preserves interpretive pluralism: conserving the commitment kernel does not resolve contested statutory interpretations but ensures those interpretations survive AI transformation intact.
