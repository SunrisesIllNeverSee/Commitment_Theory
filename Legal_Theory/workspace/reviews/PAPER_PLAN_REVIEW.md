## Legal Theory Track Summary

**Overall:** Coherent 11-paper program, correctly sequenced, citation chain internally consistent.

**Four track-level risks:**

1. **L-002 corpus bottleneck** — L-002 is the empirical spine for L-003, L-005, L-007, L-010. Corpus described as "needed" but not yet planned. THIS IS THE MOST URGENT GAP. If summer 2026 corpus construction slips, fall 2026 papers cannot be submitted on schedule.

2. **Collaborator dependencies** — Three papers require collaborators not yet identified: L-002 (beneficial), L-008 (essential), L-010 (primary author). Identifying legal collaborators — especially L-008 and L-010 — must be treated as a milestone, not a background task.

3. **Paper 0 peer review status** — L-006, L-007, L-010 make claims that depend on Paper 0 being peer-reviewed. Daubert analysis in L-010 requires actual peer review. Multiple papers should be held until Paper 0 clears peer review.

4. **"CT is unique/only/first" claim family** — L-000, L-003, L-007, L-008 all contain variants. Consistent defensible formulation across all: "first AI governance framework grounded in an empirically falsifiable conservation law." All plans should adopt this before any submission.

**Track strengths:** Doctrinal architecture (Mathews / FRE 901-Daubert / Griggs) is correctly deployed. Pluralism-preservation claim distinguishes CT from technocratic AI governance proposals. Failure mode taxonomy is the most practitioner-accessible contribution. L-001 timing is well-positioned in the developing AI civil rights enforcement literature.
