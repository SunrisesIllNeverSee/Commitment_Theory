## I. Introduction (~1,200 words)

**Opening:** Semantic continuity assumption in law — stable meaning is the water legal practice swims in.

**The shift:** AI transforms language. The question is whether retention can be verified.

**Framework claim:** Conservation Law of Commitment. In physics, a conservation law describes an invariant under transformation. CT's Conservation Law of Commitment describes such an invariant: C(T_gov(S)) = C(S).

**Pluralism (paragraph 3):** The framework does not resolve interpretive disputes. It preserves whatever meaning was committed. Both courts' readings preserved as distinct kernels. Record integrity, not adjudication.

**Gap:** No existing framework governs the transformation itself — no principle ensures legal meaning survives the AI pipeline.

**Roadmap:** Six Parts.

## V. Objections and Replies (~1,500 words)

Five-objection structure drawn from stress-test analysis:
1. "Conservation law" is premature — specific scientific category (invariant under transformation), not settled law
2. EU AI Act > a Zenodo preprint — different categories (regulation vs. physics)
3. Constitutional AI also has empirical support — effectiveness claim vs. invariance claim
4. Governance vs. science conflation — both needed; CT provides physics for what regulation prescribes
5. Proprietary = rent-seeking — law is open; machine is proprietary (Daubert analogy)
6. Framework too rigid — preserves pluralism, does not arbitrate
7. Blackhole destroys evidence — metabolizes and re-emits; safeguard not deletion
